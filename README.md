## Email Marketing Fundamentals for Creators

### The Creator Email Stack

Most creators need just three things:

1. **An email platform** (ConvertKit, Beehiiv, or Mailchimp)
2. **A lead magnet** (free resource that attracts subscribers)
3. **An automated welcome sequence** (turns subscribers into fans/customers)

That's it. You don't need complex funnels, expensive tools, or a marketing degree. The templates in this repository give you everything for steps 2 and 3.

### The Email Value Equation

Every email you send should satisfy this equation:

```
Value Delivered > Time to Read
```

If your subscriber gains more than they spend in attention, they'll keep opening. If not, they'll unsubscribe. It's that simple.

### Frequency Guidelines

| List Size | Recommended Frequency | Why |
|-----------|----------------------|-----|
| 0-1,000 | 1-2x per week | Build relationship, find your voice |
| 1,000-5,000 | 2-3x per week | Increase touchpoints, test what works |
| 5,000-20,000 | 3-5x per week | Maximize revenue, segment actively |
| 20,000+ | Daily or segmented | Different segments, different cadences |

## Building Your Email List from Zero

### Lead Magnet Ideas by Niche

A lead magnet is the free resource you offer in exchange for an email address. The best lead magnets are:
- **Specific** (solves one clear problem)
- **Quick to consume** (PDF, checklist, template -- not a 90-minute course)
- **Immediately useful** (they can use it today)
- **Related to your paid product** (natural stepping stone)

**Content Creators / Social Media:**
- Instagram caption templates (50 fill-in-the-blank captions)
- Content calendar template (30 days pre-planned)
- Hashtag strategy cheat sheet
- Hook line swipe file (grab it free: [200+ Hooks](https://wedgemethod.gumroad.com/l/free-hooks))

- Product launch checklist
- Sales page copy template
- Email sequence swipe file
- Revenue tracker template

**Coaches / Service Providers:**
- Free assessment or audit template
- Industry report or data sheet
- Decision-making framework

**Food / Recipe Creators:**

1. **Instagram bio link** -- Your bio link should go to your opt-in page
2. **Instagram Stories** -- Mention it weekly with a direct link
3. **Carousel last slide** -- End every carousel with "DM me [KEYWORD] for the free [resource]"
4. **Reels CTA** -- "Link in bio for the free [resource]"
5. **Pinterest pins** -- Create pins that link to your opt-in page
6. **YouTube description** -- First link in every video description
7. **Blog posts** -- Content upgrades within relevant articles
8. **Guest posts/podcasts** -- Always have a specific freebie to mention
9. **Cross-promotions** -- Partner with creators at your level to swap promos
10. **Comment engagement** -- When someone asks a question your lead magnet answers, mention it

### A/B Testing Framework

Always test your subject lines. Here's the system:

1. **Write 5 subject line options** for every email
2. **Pick the top 2** based on your gut + the formulas above
3. **A/B test** by sending each to 15% of your list
4. **Wait 2 hours** for results
5. **Send the winner** to the remaining 70%

**What to test:**
- Curiosity vs. benefit (which performs better for YOUR audience?)
- Short (3-5 words) vs. long (8-12 words)
- With numbers vs. without
- With emoji vs. without
- With name personalization vs. without
- Question vs. statement

## Segmentation Strategies

Segmentation is how you send the right message to the right person. Here are the segments every creator should build:

### Essential Segments

| Segment | How to Create | What to Send |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| New Subscribers (< 30 days) | Automated tag on join | Welcome sequence, foundational content |
| Engaged (opened 3+ of last 5) | Open/click activity | Launch emails, premium offers |
| Disengaged (no open in 60 days) | Inactivity trigger | Re-engagement sequence, then prune |
| Customers | Purchase tag | Upsells, loyalty offers, feedback requests |
| Non-Customers | No purchase tag | Nurture, social proof, entry-level offers |
| By Lead Magnet | Tag by which freebie | Topic-specific content and offers |
| By Interest | Survey or click behavior | Relevant content only |

### How to Segment Without Being Creepy

1. **Ask directly** -- Use a welcome email survey: "What's your biggest challenge?"
2. **Track clicks** -- Tag people based on what links they click
3. **Use lead magnet origin** -- Different freebies = different interests
4. **Purchase behavior** -- What they buy tells you what they need next
5. **Engagement level** -- Send more to people who open more

## Automation Workflows

### 1. New Subscriber Automation

```
Trigger: New subscriber
  → Tag with lead magnet source
  → Add to Welcome Sequence
  → Wait 7 days
  → If opened 3+ emails:
      → Tag as "Engaged"
      → Add to Evergreen Funnel
  → If opened <2 emails:
      → Tag as "Needs Nurture"
      → Send 3 value-only emails
      → Re-evaluate engagement
```

### 2. Abandoned Cart Recovery

```
Trigger: Clicked product link but didn't purchase (24 hours)
  → Email 1: "Still thinking about [product]?"
    (Address top objection + testimonial)
  → Wait 24 hours
  → If no purchase:
    → Email 2: "Quick question about [product]"
      (Ask what's holding them back)
  → Wait 48 hours
  → If no purchase:
    → Email 3: "[Discount code] to make the decision easier"
      (Small additional incentive)
```

### 3. Re-Engagement Campaign

```
Trigger: No email opens in 60 days
  → Email 1: "Are we still good?"
    Subject: "Should I stop emailing you?"
    (Give them a clear "stay" or "go" option)
  → Wait 5 days
  → If no open:
    → Email 2: "Last email from me (unless you say so)"
      (Final chance with a compelling offer or resource)
  → Wait 5 days
  → If no open:
    → Remove from active list
    → Tag as "Churned"
    → (Saves deliverability and money)
```

### 4. Post-Purchase Nurture

```
Trigger: Product purchased
  → Email 1 (immediately): Purchase confirmation + quick-start guide
  → Email 2 (Day 2): "How to get the most out of [product]"
  → Email 3 (Day 5): Check-in + request for feedback
  → Email 4 (Day 14): Results check + testimonial request
  → Email 5 (Day 30): Upsell to next product in ladder
```

## Email Copywriting Frameworks

### AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

```
[ATTENTION: Hook that grabs them]
[INTEREST: Expand on the hook, make it relevant to them]
[DESIRE: Paint the outcome, social proof, emotional pull]
[ACTION: Clear CTA -- one action, one link]
```

### PAS (Problem-Agitation-Solution)

```
[PROBLEM: Name their pain point specifically]
[AGITATION: Make it worse -- consequences, emotions, urgency]
[SOLUTION: Your product/method as the answer]
```

### BAB (Before-After-Bridge)

```
[BEFORE: Where they are now -- the struggle]
[AFTER: Where they could be -- the dream state]
[BRIDGE: Your product/method connects the two]
```

### The 4 Ps (Promise-Picture-Proof-Push)

```
[PROMISE: What you'll deliver]
[PICTURE: Help them visualize having it]
[PROOF: Evidence it works -- testimonials, data, results]
[PUSH: CTA with urgency or incentive]
```

### The One-Sentence Email

Sometimes the most effective email is the shortest:

```
Subject: Quick question

Hey [FIRST_NAME],

What's the #1 thing holding you back from [desired result] right now?

Hit reply -- I read everything.

[YOUR NAME]
```

This email consistently gets the highest reply rates of any format. Use it to learn about your audience and generate content ideas.

## Deliverability and Technical Setup

### Authentication (Do This First)

| Protocol | What It Does | Priority |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| SPF | Authorizes sending servers | Required |
| DKIM | Cryptographically signs emails | Required |
| DMARC | Tells receivers how to handle auth failures | Recommended |

Most email platforms handle SPF and DKIM automatically. DMARC requires a DNS record.

### Deliverability Best Practices

1. **Use a custom domain** (you@yourbrand.com, not you@gmail.com)
2. **Warm up new domains** -- Start with 50 emails/day, increase by 50 every day
3. **Clean your list quarterly** -- Remove bounces and 90-day inactive subscribers
4. **Use double opt-in** for international lists (required by GDPR for EU subscribers)
5. **Include an unsubscribe link** in every email (it's the law)
6. **Don't use all caps or excessive exclamation points** in subject lines
7. **Keep image-to-text ratio at 60/40** (more text than images)
8. **Avoid spam trigger words**: "free money," "act now," "limited time" in subject lines
9. **Send consistently** -- Irregular sending patterns trigger spam filters
10. **Monitor your sender reputation** at Google Postmaster Tools

### List Hygiene Protocol

Run this quarterly:
1. Export subscribers who haven't opened in 90 days
2. Send a re-engagement campaign (3 emails over 10 days)
3. Anyone who doesn't re-engage gets removed
4. This improves your open rates, click rates, and deliverability

## Common Email Marketing Mistakes

### 1. Not Starting Your Email List

The best time to start was when you created your first piece of content. The second best time is today. Every day without a list is lost subscribers and lost revenue.

### 2. Treating Email Like Social Media

Email is intimate. It's in their personal inbox. Write like you're emailing a friend, not broadcasting to a crowd. Use "you" and "I," not "we" and "our audience."

### 3. Only Emailing When You Want to Sell

If you only email during launches, your list goes cold between promotions. Email consistently with value, so when you do sell, they're warm and trusting.

### 4. Writing Boring Subject Lines

Your subject line competes with dozens of other emails. It needs to be compelling enough to earn a click. Use the formulas above and always A/B test.

### 5. Having No Clear CTA

Every email should have ONE primary call-to-action. Not three links to different things. One clear action you want them to take.

### 6. Ignoring Mobile

65%+ of emails are opened on mobile. Use short paragraphs (1-3 sentences), plenty of white space, and buttons instead of text links.

### 7. Not Segmenting

Sending the same email to everyone is leaving money on the table. At minimum, segment by engagement level and customer status.

### 8. Forgetting the Welcome Sequence

Your welcome sequence does the heavy lifting of turning strangers into fans. Without it, new subscribers go cold within 48 hours.

### 9. Not Cleaning Your List

A bloated list full of inactive subscribers hurts your deliverability and costs you money. Clean quarterly.

### 10. Giving Up Too Early

Email marketing compounds over time. The first 500 subscribers are the hardest. By 2,000 you'll see consistent results. By 5,000 you'll wonder why you didn't start sooner.
